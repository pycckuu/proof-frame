technical architecture

Seven steps from capture to verification

Step 1
Camera signs
Hardware secure element creates ECDSA signature over file hash. Proves physical device, not AI.
Step 2
ZK proof
RISC Zero zkVM verifies sig, decodes image, applies transforms, hashes pixels. All private.
Step 3
World ID
Orb-verified human bound to this image. Anti-Sybil. Unlinkable nullifier per attestation.
Step 4
Ledger signs
ERC-7730 Clear Signing displays attestation fields on Ledger screen. EIP-712 typed data. Physical button press.
Step 5
On-chain
Relayer submits proofs. Contract verifies ZK proof + World ID. Stores pixelHash + IPFS CID. Creates ENS subdomain.
Step 6
ENS records
On-chain subdomain via NameWrapper. Text records: pixelHash, fileHash, ipfsCid, url, avatar, contenthash.
Step 7
Verify
Anyone: upload image → hash pixels → check contract or ENS → verified or not.

Key insight for judges

AI can generate photorealistic images, but cannot forge a hardware camera signature. ProofFrame moves the trust anchor from "who posted this" to "what device captured this" — and ZK proofs verify that without revealing the device owner.
